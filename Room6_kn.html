The Sprouted Coconut's Second Life

You know how a coconut starts growing a little green shoot when it begins to sprout? Well, Tulu people found a clever use for these sprouted coconuts!They would take the coconut, remove the outer husk, and make a hole in it. Then, using a stick, they'd carefully scrape out all the soft coconut flesh from inside. What remained was a perfect natural container!

The Perfect Spice Keeper

Why was this coconut shell so good for storing spices?

  • It kept the spices dry
  • It protected them from insects
  • It was completely natural
  • It had a natural lid that could be made to close tight
  • The coconut smell actually mixed nicely with the spices!

What Went Inside?

Tulu families would store their most important cooking spices:

  • Coriander seeds (for that fresh, citrusy flavor)
  • Cumin (for earthy warmth)
  • Fenugreek (slightly bitter, very healthy)
  • Pepper (for that kick!)
  • And many other secret family spices!
